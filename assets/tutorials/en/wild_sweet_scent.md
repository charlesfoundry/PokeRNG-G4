# HGSS Wild Sweet Scent

## Core Idea

Wild grass encounters use different methods:

- HGSS: Method K
- DPPt: Method J

Before searching, confirm:

- game version
- location
- time period
- lead ability
- encounter slot conditions

## Areas With NPCs

If the area has moving or turning NPCs, load the save and open the menu as quickly as possible to freeze them.

Then try to complete these from inside the menu:

- seed verification by phone
- Chatot frame advances
- using Sweet Scent from the Pokemon party menu

Wild areas with NPCs may be less stable than stationary Pokemon or Honey Trees. If offsets are inconsistent, prefer a quieter location.

## HGSS Phone Verification

In HGSS, Professor Elm and Juggler Irwin calls can verify the seed.

Each call advances **1 frame**, so those calls must be counted as part of the current frame.

Flow:

1. Open the app seed search page.
2. After each phone call, tap the exact matching E/K/P result in the same order.
3. Confirm the target seed.
4. Manually tap the seed result that was hit.
5. Continue with Chatot advances as shown by the app.

If you made 6 calls on the real game, tap 6 matching E/K/P results in the app.

After confirming the hit seed, you must tap that seed result so the app can calculate the remaining frame advances.

If you did not hit the target seed, you still need to tap the seed you actually hit. The app returns to the calibration page and fills the delay actually hit in this attempt.

The timer is updated only after you tap Apply Calibration.

If the hit delay differs from the target delay by only **±2**, you can avoid updating calibration yet and try a few more times with the current calibration first. If the same offset appears consistently, then apply the calibration.

## Triggering the Encounter

After confirming the seed:

1. Use Chatot to advance onto the target frame.
2. Use Sweet Scent or Honey on the target frame.
3. Catch the Pokemon.
4. Record its stats.

Sweet Scent does not advance to the next frame before generating the encounter.

The wild Pokemon is generated from the **current frame** at the moment the encounter is triggered.

## Offset Check

If the captured Pokemon lands on a different frame, enter its stats into reverse search on the calibration page.

The actual frame can show whether the location and action path have a stable offset.
